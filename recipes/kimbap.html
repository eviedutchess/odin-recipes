KimBap

Description

Kimbap is a Korean dish made from cooked rice and ingredients such as vegetables, fish, and meats that are rolled in dried sheets of seaweed and served in bite-sized slices. The dish is often part of a packed meal to be eaten at picnics and outdoor events, and can serve as a light lunch. It is a popular take-out food in South Korea and abroad and is known as a convenient food because of its portability.

'Kim' refers to edible seaweed and 'bap' broadly refers to cooked rice. Kim and bap are the two basic components of kimbap. Some varieies of kimbap include cheese, spicy cooked squid, kimchi, luncheon meat, pepper, or spicy tuna. Fillings vary, often with vegetarian and vegan options.

Ingredients

  • dried seaweed
  • cooked rice
  • sesame oil
  • anything else you want to add for the filling

Steps

  1. Tip in your sesame oil to your hot rice and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Spread the prepared rice on half the seaweed and carefully lay your filling over the rice.
  3. Roll and squeeze. A bit of extra rice smudged near the end helps it to stick.
  4. Brush your entire roll and knife with sesame oil. Then slice your roll with sharp and deliberate cuts.
